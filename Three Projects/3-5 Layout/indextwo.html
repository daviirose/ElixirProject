GETTING OUT OF YOUR COMFORT ZONE

"While despite not being very involved in high school, I still managed to form a tight niche of friends;
but I still yearned to belong to a group that made me feel bigger than myself.

Unfortunately, I consider myself virtually talentless — I can’t sing, I have two left feet, and I am pretty
much the most uncoordinated person you will ever meet. So if I wasn’t in school, I was found working
part time at Rite Aid. Once I graduated, I made a promise to myself that college was going to be
different — I would do more than just work, go to class, hang out with my friends, and watch Netflix."

During my freshman orientation, they mentioned an Activities and Involvement Fair where student
clubs and organizations would be highlighting what they were about. I was so excited! This was the
perfect opportunity for me to keep my promise — and a few days later, that’s exactly what I did.
